This data, taken from the FBI's official crime data, discusses property crime data. According to the FBI's property crime website:

"In the FBIâ€™s Uniform Crime Reporting (UCR) Program, property crime includes the offenses of burglary, larceny-theft, motor vehicle theft, and arson. The object of the theft-type offenses is the taking of money or property, but there is no force or threat of force against the victims."

In general, property crime is not life-threatening, but may vary in severity from misdemeanor offenses to felonies based upon value of property damaged or amount of property affected in the crime. The first chart shows a trend in the past ten years in the U.S. that shows that the number of property crimes committed has in fact been in a downward trend in crimes reported, despite the fact that the population of the United States has solidly increased. The second chart shows a breakdown in 2014's statistics based upon property crime statistics reported in the FBI's four major regions of the United States.
